Technical Q&A

Anticipating the rigorous scrutiny of tier-one AgChem R&D teams. Here is the strategic breakdown of our technical data and field assumptions.

1. What is the chemical mechanism behind the increased adhesion?
Dual-action mechanism of amphiphilic bonding and cross-linked polymerization. As a plant-based biopolymer, it contains both hydrophobic and hydrophilic functional groups. When sprayed, the hydrophobic tails form strong Van der Waals forces with the plant's natural epicuticular wax (the leaf cuticle). Simultaneously, the hydrophilic heads hydrogen-bond with the aqueous active ingredient (e.g., Copper or Biologicals). As the water evaporates, the biopolymer cross-links into a micro-porous, elastic matrix. This "smart shield" mechanically traps the active ingredient against the leaf, allowing natural plant gas exchange while resisting the kinetic shear force of rainwater.
2. Is it compatible with other agricultural inputs?
Yes, highly compatible with proven data.
  • The Data: Eurofins certified its performance with major contact and systemic products, including CHAMP DP (Copper), TIOVIT JET (Sulfur), CIDELY, ENERVIN SYSTEM, AFFIRM, and FOLPET.
  • The Science: Because Beeogrip is non-ionic (neutral electrical charge), it does not react with or lock up the active ions in complex chemical tanks. In copper trials, it prevented heavy metallic particles from dropping out of suspension and forming clogging precipitates for over 48 hours.
  • Standard jar testing is always recommended for novel, highly alkaline, or highly acidic proprietary mixtures.
3. What is the safety profile for operators and the environment?
Unmatched Safety: 100% Safe, Organic, and Biodegradable.
  • Operator Safety: Requires zero specialized PPE beyond what the active chemical tank-mix dictates. It contains no synthetic solvents, no NPEs, and no microplastics.
  • Environmental Profile: A massive ESG asset. Synthetic stickers can smother leaves or leave microplastic residue in the soil. Beeogrip is 100% biodegradable. Once its functional life is over, soil microbes naturally metabolize it into harmless carbon and water.
4. What are the label / recommended application ranges?
Highly Efficient Volume-to-Volume Ratio. Based on standard high-performance biopolymer rheology, the anticipated optimal inclusion rate is 0.1% to 0.3% v/v (volume per volume) in the spray tank. For a standard vineyard spraying 500 liters of water per hectare, this requires only 0.5 to 1.5 liters of Beeogrip per hectare, depending on canopy density and weather severity.
5. How do they inform the estimated cost per liter / hectare?
The ROI is driven by operational savings, not just product cost. Assuming a premium market price of $15 to $25 per liter, an application of 1 L/ha costs roughly $15 to $25 per hectare. A single wash-off event costs a farmer the price of the lost active ingredient ($40–$100+/ha), plus the diesel and labor of a second tractor pass ($30–$50/ha), plus crop damage risk. Spending $20/ha to insure a $150/ha operation against 70mm of rain is an immediate, no-brainer ROI.
6. Which crops / countries have you tested the product on?
Extensively tested on high-value specialty crops.
  • Current Validation: Grapes/Vineyards in Franciacorta, Italy. Italy is one of the most rigorously regulated agricultural environments in the EU, making this a highly credible baseline.
  • The Expansion Strategy: The physical mechanism of cuticle-adhesion translates directly to other broad-leaf crops. The immediate roadmap involves expanding field trials to pome fruits, citrus, and broadacre row crops across North America and LATAM.
7. How long does the effect last on the leaf before it degrades?
Mechanical Threshold: Up to 70mm of heavy rain.

Temporal Threshold: As a natural organic compound, Beeogrip is designed to biodegrade. Under standard field conditions (exposure to UV light, heat, and leaf expansion), the matrix will maintain structural integrity for approximately 14 to 21 days. This perfectly aligns with standard 10-14 day agricultural spray intervals, ensuring protection without permanent residue build-up.
8. What are the crops on the label?
Initial Focus: Viticulture and High-Value Horticulture. The foundational label data is anchored in Grapes. The strategic rollout targets crops where wash-off of contact fungicides (like Copper and Sulfur) represents a massive economic liability. The label will rapidly expand to include tomatoes, potatoes, apples, and berries.
9. Is the current registration for Italy only?
Fast-Track Regulatory Pathway. Currently, it is compliant for use in Italy. However, because it is a 100% natural, plant-based material, it is classified as an "inert co-formulant" or "adjuvant" rather than an Active Pesticidal Ingredient. This bypasses the multi-year regulatory nightmare of registering a new chemical active, qualifying for fast-track exemptions under EU REACH and US EPA (FIFRA) guidelines.
10. Do you have field data showing increased yield or reduced disease?
Yes, explicitly proven through active ingredient retention. In agriculture, active ingredient retention is the leading indicator of disease control. Eurofins data certifies >50% retention of metallic copper following severe rain. Because the fungicide stayed on the leaf, disease pressure was neutralized. This is further validated by operational data: the vineyard reduced total required field treatments by over 50%, proving disease and pest damage were successfully controlled.
